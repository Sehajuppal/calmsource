# CalmSource Experience Strategy

## Product idea

CalmSource is not a catalog with a player attached. It is the shortest path from
“I want to watch something” to confident playback across personal IPTV and
Stremio-style sources.

The interface should feel calm even when the underlying source graph is complex.
People choose content first; CalmSource chooses and explains the best source.
Technical control remains available through progressive disclosure.

## Experience principles

1. **Resume before rediscover.** In-progress content and the last live channel are
   the fastest paths back into entertainment and should remain easy to reach.
2. **Show why, not everything.** Recommendations need a short, human reason.
   Source diagnostics belong behind Source Controls, not in the primary journey.
3. **One obvious next action.** Details lead with Play or Resume. Secondary actions
   never compete visually with playback.
4. **Content is the surface.** Artwork, typography, motion, and focus create the
   hierarchy. Permanent chrome stays quiet.
5. **Live is a mode, not a data type.** “Now” and “Next,” favorites, recent
   channels, and a predictable guide matter more than provider boundaries.
6. **Trust through legibility.** Never expose or persist credentials and raw URLs.
   Present quality, language, reliability, and source type in plain language.
7. **TV and mobile share intent, not layout.** TV optimizes for low-attention D-pad
   consumption; mobile optimizes for quick scanning, search, and list management.

## Information architecture

- **Home:** one featured decision, Continue Watching, then focused personalized
  rows. Avoid duplicate rows and decorative categories without a distinct intent.
- **Live:** favorites and recent channels first, then a unified now/next guide.
- **My Space:** in progress, saved, recent live, history, and prior searches.
- **Search:** title, person, genre, channel, mood, or natural phrase. Results are
  visual and grouped by intent.
- **Details:** Play/Resume, My List, synopsis and episodes, related titles, Ways to
  Watch, then Source Controls.
- **Player:** content first; controls appear on intent and disappear quickly.

## Feature priorities

### Now

- Cinematic Top Shelf and poster-first browsing
- Strong, stable TV focus and focus restoration
- Visual search results with mood prompts
- My Space return dashboard
- Play/Resume-first details with progressive source disclosure
- Unified live guide with favorites and recent channels

### Next

- Long-press “More like this / Not for me” taste feedback
- First-run taste calibration using a small title set
- Natural-language and voice search mapped onto the existing search engine
- “Because you watched…” and time-sensitive recommendation reasons
- A compact “Up next” player sheet and clearer episode continuation
- Android TV Watch Next / launcher recommendations

### Later

- Optional ambient artwork mode for idle TV
- Household-aware shared recommendations
- Cross-device handoff using privacy-safe media identifiers

## Research basis

- Apple Human Interface Guidelines: tvOS immersion, edge-to-edge artwork, subtle
  motion, and clear focus.
- Amazon Fire TV UX guidance: low information density, important content first,
  consumption-first flows, and an explicit 10-foot safe zone.
- Netflix 2025 TV redesign: decision-relevant metadata, visible Search/My List,
  and recommendations responsive to current intent.
- Google TV: a unified watchlist, personalized profiles, and live content organized
  independently of provider boundaries.
- Spotify: mood- and moment-based discovery instead of genre-only browsing.
- Nielsen/Gracenote: discovery difficulty and fragmentation directly reduce
  streaming satisfaction.
